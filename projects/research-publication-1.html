Characterizing Opinion Evolution of Networked LLMs

Caleb Probine, Yigit Ege Bayiz, Filippos Fotiadis, Samuel Li, Yunhao Yang, Ufuk Topcu

Submitted to EMNLP 2026

Code

Summary

LLMs are increasingly appearing in multi-agent simulations, influence settings, and AI-native social platforms, where their responses can shape one another. We ask whether classical opinion-dynamics models provide a principled way to explain and forecast how stances evolve in networks of interacting LLM agents.

Finding

Basic averaging models miss a key part of the behavior. Across model families, discussion topics, and network structures, adding a model-specific innate opinion bias explains the observed stance trajectories much better, reducing mean opinion error by up to 88%.

My role

My work focused on the simulation and modeling pipeline: generating networked LLM discussions, logging posts, stances, and topology data, and fitting opinion-dynamics models to compare predicted and observed stance trajectories.
